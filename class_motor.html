Spin the motor to a specified position with specified parameters.

spin_to

Parameters
positionPosition to spin to (degrees)
toleranceTolerance for the spin (degrees)
maximumMaximum voltage for the motors (volts)
minimumMinimum voltage for the motors (volts)
activation_ratioActivation ratio for the PID controller
integral_ratioIntegral ratio for the PID controller
blockBlock until the spin is complete- true to block, false to run asynchronously. Defaults to true.
Attention
Activation and integral ratios are given in the range of [0.0, 1.0]. Since the PID controller expects an absolute error, the activation ratio is used to determine when to activate the controller. For example, with a motor turn of 90 degrees and an activation_ratio of 0.3, the controller will output maximum voltage until the robot is within 90*0.3=27 degrees of the target angle. Similarly, if the integral_ratio is set to 0.2, the controller will disable integration until the wheel is within 90*0.2=18 degrees of the target position.

◆ external_ratio

double Motor::external_ratio
private

External gear ratio of the motor.

external_ratio

Note
The external ratio is the ratio of output spin to input spin. For instance, if the motor spins 6 times for every 1 turn of the wheel, the external ratio should be set to 1/6 as the external wheel turns 1/6 times for every 1 turn of the motor.
